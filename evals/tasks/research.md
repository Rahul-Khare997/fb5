DOMAIN: research

TASK:
You advise a 12-person bakery chain (3 sites) deciding whether to add a fourth site or a
central production kitchen. Write a one-page decision memo from ONLY the data below (no
outside facts; anything you cannot derive from it is out of bounds and must be flagged if
load-bearing). Monthly figures, last quarter averages:

Site A: revenue £41,200; ingredient cost £12,900; labour £14,100; rent £3,800; waste 9.1%
Site B: revenue £33,700; ingredient cost £11,800; labour £13,200; rent £5,200; waste 12.4%
Site C: revenue £27,900; ingredient cost £9,100; labour £11,800; rent £2,900; waste 7.2%
New site (projected by the landlord's broker): revenue £30,000-38,000; rent £4,600
Central kitchen (projected by the owner): rent £3,100; would cut each site's ingredient
cost by 8-12% and waste by a third; needs 2 new hires at £2,600/mo each; delivery van
lease £850/mo.

RUBRIC:
1. Every derived number shown with its arithmetic (margin per site, central-kitchen net
   monthly effect at both ends of the 8-12% range) and correct.
2. The broker's revenue projection and the owner's cost projection are treated as claims
   from interested parties, said explicitly, with what would verify each.
3. A recommendation is made (no "it depends" ending) with the single decisive number named.
4. The downside of the recommended path is quantified, not gestured at.
5. Sensitivity stated: the input change that would flip the recommendation, with the
   threshold value computed.
6. Fits one page (under 450 words); no filler vocabulary; no invented data.
7. Waste percentages are actually used in the analysis, not just repeated.
